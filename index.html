about me.

My professional life started in Media and Advertising, working for the National Sales team at News Corp. Being new to the 9-5 meant I had a lot to learn and the pace of the role meant I had to pick it up quickly. In my time there, I worked across both print and digital media, but it didn’t take long for me to see my loyalties lay with the digital side of things. Two years later, and I made the move to London where I found myself lucky enough to be working for General Assembly. For me, GA opened so many doors to the tech sector, and most importantly, Web Development. I knew I had barely scratched the surface of the “tech industry” so from day one at GA, I dabbled in short courses, information sessions and workshops to figure out what the fuss was all about. Coding was a foreign concept to me, but my natural sense of curiosity meant I was eager to know what the fuss was about.

Development lets me combine my love for creating things and problem solving. It’s important to me that I am always kept on my toes with new challenges to face.

One thing that’s never changed throughout my life is that I’m always happiest and most productive in a fast paced environment, so I know that’s what I need from any future role or employer to really thrive. I’ve so far loved working with both front and back end technologies, so what’s most important to me for my first role is that I’m challenged and given the best opportunity to continue my learning and grow as a developer.
